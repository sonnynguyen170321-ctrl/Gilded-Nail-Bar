# Gilded Nail Bar — Brand Guidelines

**Version 1.0 — June 2026**

---

## 01 — Brand Foundation

### 1.1 Brand Essence

Gilded Nail Bar is a sanctuary of raw luxury — where the unadorned meets the precious. We sit at the intersection of wabi-sabi minimalism and bespoke opulence, offering nail artistry that honors natural form while indulging in rare materials and masterful technique.

### 1.2 Brand Pillars

| Pillar | Description |
|---|---|
| **Raw Luxury** | Luxury stripped of pretension. Natural materials, honest textures, quiet opulence. |
| **Apothecary Craft** | Treatments rooted in botanical ingredients, formulated with the precision of an old-world apothecary. |
| **Artisan Precision** | Every service is a bespoke creation. Russian dry technique, hand-placed gold leaf, sculptural gel work. |
| **Sacred Ritual** | The appointment is a ritual, not a transaction. Stillness, attention, sensory care. |

### 1.3 Brand Persona

If Gilded Nail Bar were a person, they would be:
- **Archetype:** The Alchemist / The Curator
- **Character:** Quietly confident, discerning, warm but reserved. Speaks in carefully chosen words. Dressed in raw silk and aged leather. Collects Japanese ceramics and rare botanical extracts.
- **Aspirational References:** Le Labo (apothecary minimalism), C.O. Bigelow (heritage apothecary), Aesop (sensory formulations), Rick Owens (raw architectural forms), Axel Vervoordt (wabi-sabi interiors).

### 1.4 Mission Statement

To provide a sanctuary for hands and mind through precision nail artistry, botanical care, and an environment that honors stillness, material honesty, and the beauty of imperfection.

### 1.5 Target Audience

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| **Primary** | Women 28–55, HHI $150K+, residing in Uptown / Knox / Turtle Creek Dallas. Professionals, creatives, and socials who value design, craft, and discretion. |
| **Secondary** | Men seeking precision dry manicures. Bridal parties for private events. Brand collaborators (fashion editorials, trunk shows). |
| **Psychographic** | Values quality over quantity. Seeks experiences over transactions. Drawn to raw textures, muted palettes, typographic rigor. Reads Kinfolk, Cereal, Monocle. |

## 02 — Logo & Wordmark

### 2.1 Primary Logo

The Gilded Nail Bar logo is a custom logotype set in an elegant serif-inspired letterform with subtle calligraphic flourishes. The wordmark is the primary identifier and should always be the first choice for applications.

- **Primary version:** Full logotype centered or left-aligned
- **Minimum size (print):** 1.5 in / 38 mm wide
- **Minimum size (digital):** 60 px wide
- **Clear space:** Equal to the height of the letter "G" in the logo

### 2.2 Logo Variations

| Variation | Use Case |
|---|---|
| **Full Color** (bronze on cream) | Primary — all standard applications |
| **Solid Bronze** (`#584638`) | Single-color print, foil stamping, embossing |
| **Solid Cream** (`#f8efe1`) | On dark backgrounds, photography overlays |
| **Black** | Monochrome print, fax, low-resolution |

### 2.3 Logo Misuse

Never:
- Stretch, skew, rotate, or distort the logo
- Change the logo colors outside approved variations
- Add drop shadows, outlines, or effects
- Place on low-contrast backgrounds
- Rearrange or separate wordmark elements
- Place inside a shape or container
- Apply to busy photography without sufficient contrast overlay

### 2.4 Vertical Lockup (Alternate)

A stacked vertical lockup exists for square applications (social avatars, stickers, favicon). The "Gilded" sits above a thin rule, with "Nail Bar" beneath. Use only when horizontal lockup is impractical.

## 04 — Typography

### 4.1 Typeface Family

Three typefaces form the Gilded typographic system:

| Typeface | Weight Range | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Cormorant Garamond** | Light 300, Regular 400, Medium 500, Semibold 600, Italic variants | Headings, display, pull quotes, editorial — the brand voice |
| **IBM Plex Mono** | Light 300, Regular 400, Medium 500, Semibold 600 | UI labels, prices, metadata, step indicators, all-caps accents |
| **Inter** | Light 300, Regular 400, Medium 500, Semibold 600 | Body copy, descriptions, form inputs, navigation |

### 4.2 Type Hierarchy

```
Level 1 — Hero Display
  Cormorant Garamond, Italic, 2.8rem / 3.2rem
  Letter-spacing: -0.01em
  Weight: 500 (Medium)

Level 2 — Section Title
  Cormorant Garamond, Italic, 2.5rem / 2.8rem
  Letter-spacing: -0.01em
  Weight: 500 (Medium)

Level 3 — Card / Component Title
  Cormorant Garamond, Italic, 1.4rem / 1.6rem
  Weight: 500 (Medium)

Level 4 — Subtitle / Meta
  IBM Plex Mono, Uppercase, 0.75rem / 1rem
  Letter-spacing: 0.2em
  Weight: 400 (Regular)

Level 5 — Body Copy
  Inter, 0.95rem / 1.6
  Weight: 400 (Regular)

Level 6 — Labels / Captions
  IBM Plex Mono, Uppercase, 0.65rem / 0.9rem
  Letter-spacing: 0.1em
  Weight: 400 (Regular)

Level 7 — Pricing / Data
  IBM Plex Mono, 0.95rem / 1.2
  Weight: 500 (Medium)
```

### 4.3 Type Rules

- **No** typefaces outside the specified three. No system fonts, no decorative fonts, no variable fonts.
- **No** letter-spacing on Cormorant Garamond headings (except -0.01em).
- **All-caps** only with IBM Plex Mono — never with Cormorant or Inter.
- **Line length:** 55–75 characters per line for body text.
- **Italic** is reserved for Cormorant Garamond headings and pull quotes only.
- Never use faux-bold, faux-italic, or any synthetic typeface styling.

### 4.4 Leading / Line Height

| Element | Line Height |
|---|---|
| Display headings | 1.1 |
| Section titles | 1.15 |
| Body text | 1.6 |
| Mono labels | 1.3 |

## 05 — Voice & Tone

### 5.1 Brand Voice Principles

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| **Sparse** | Fewer words, greater impact. Every word earns its place. |
| **Sensory** | Describe textures, temperatures, light, and materials. Evoke the physical. |
| **Precise** | "Hand-placed 24k liquid gold foil leaf" not "gold stickers." Technical accuracy is part of the luxury. |
| **Warm** | Never cold or corporate. The warmth of the cream silk background carries into the copy. |
| **Ritualistic** | Language of ceremony and care. The appointment is a ritual, the hands are honored. |

### 5.2 Do & Don't (Voice)

| Do Say | Don't Say |
|---|---|
| "A sanctuary for your hands" | "A nail salon" |
| "Hand-placed 24k liquid gold foil leaf accents" | "Gold nail stickers" |
| "Russian dry cuticle precision care" | "Cuticle trimming" |
| "Bespoke structured gel overlay" | "Gel nails" |
| "Botanical Cabinet" | "Products page" |
| "Guest" | "Customer" |
| "Reservation" | "Booking" (except in UI context) |
| "Master Artist" | "Nail tech" |

### 5.3 Tone Matrix

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Service description | Poetic, precise | "Dry cuticle precision care, structured base reinforcement, and hand-placed 24k liquid gold foil leaf accents." |
| Booking flow | Clear, warm | "Select the treatment you would like to reserve." |
| Error / validation | Direct, polite | "Please select a service before proceeding." |
| Confirmation | Celebratory, ritual | "Your reservation is confirmed. Thank you for choosing Gilded Nail Bar." |
| Social media | Intimate, editorial | "Saturday light on McKinney Ave. Gold foil, quiet hands." |
| FAQ | Helpful, thorough | "Our structured gel manicures typically last 2–3 weeks with proper aftercare." |

### 5.4 Copy Patterns

- **Section headers:** "THE SERVICE MENU", "THE NAIL ART VAULT", "THE BOTANICAL CABINET" — definite article + poetic noun
- **Subtitle pattern:** Mono label above section headings — "TREATMENTS CATALOG", "GUEST FEEDBACK"
- **Numeric identifiers:** Roman numerals for botanical ingredients (I, II, III), zero-padded step numbers (01, 02, 03)
- **Actions:** "ENTER SALON", "EXPLORE MENU", "BOOK APPOINTMENT"

### 5.5 Capitalization Rules

- **All-caps:** UI labels, buttons, navigation, section subtitles, step indicators, mono elements
- **Title Case:** Section headings (Cormorant Garamond, italic) — "The Nail Art Vault"
- **Sentence case:** Body copy, descriptions, forms

## 06 — Photography & Imagery

### 6.1 Image Direction

The Gilded visual language is raw, textured, and intimate. Images should feel editorial, not commercial.

**Subjects:**
- Close-cropped hands in natural poses — never splayed or artificial
- Hands interacting with raw materials: stone, ceramic, linen, gold leaf
- Salon interiors: concrete surfaces, natural light, linen curtains, aged brass
- Botanical ingredients in their raw state: honey, milk, salt, clay, argan oil
- Negative space — let the frame breathe

**Lighting:**
- Natural daylight only — no harsh studio strobes or ring lights
- Golden hour warmth, soft window light, directional north light
- High contrast but underexposed shadows — moody but not dark

**Post-Processing:**
- Desaturated slightly from natural (warm shift, not cool)
- Sepia tone lift in shadows (+8–12%)
- Reduced clarity / texture for skin (soft, not blurred)
- Film grain simulation (very subtle)

### 6.2 What Never to Photograph

- Fluorescent-lit salon shots
- Overhead group hands (the "Instagram nail spread")
- Hands holding phones, coffee cups, or props
- Busy backgrounds, patterned fabrics, or reflective surfaces
- Overly manicured models — real hands, real skin

### 6.3 Color Treatment

All imagery should carry the brand's warm beige undertone:
- White balance shifted +300K toward warmth
- Shadows tinted toward bronze `#584638`
- Highlights tinted toward cream `#f8efe1`
- Black point lifted slightly (no pure blacks in shadows)

### 6.4 Image Sourcing

| Source | Acceptable |
|---|---|
| Original photography | Preferred — commission editorial shoots |
| Unsplash / Pexels | Only with heavy color grading to match brand |
| Stock photography | Never — too clean, too artificial |

## 07 — Iconography

### 7.1 Icon Style

- **Line weight:** 1.5px consistent stroke
- **Corners:** Rounded (stroke-linejoin: round, stroke-linecap: round)
- **Size:** 1em base, scaled proportionally
- **Color:** CurrentColor (inherits from text), always mono
- **Style:** Minimal, geometric, no filled areas (except Pinterest, TikTok brand icons)
- **Animation:** Hover pulse or rotation on interactive icons

### 7.2 Icon Set

The brand uses a custom SVG sprite system (see `index.html` — inline `<svg>` sprite). Every icon is hand-coded, not imported from a library. No FontAwesome dependency — all FA classes are replaced at runtime with inline SVG equivalents.

### 7.3 Icon Usage Rules

- Never use emoji as icons
- Never use third-party icon libraries (FontAwesome, Material Icons, Phosphor)
- Never color icons outside the brand palette
- Icons should always communicate function without text (where possible) with ARIA labels for accessibility

## 08 — Patterns, Textures & Materials

### 8.1 Physical Materials

The brand's physical identity (salon space, print materials) should evoke:

| Material | Application |
|---|---|
| **Raw concrete** | Walls, counters, flooring |
| **Linen** | Curtains, towels, appointment cards |
| **Aged brass** | Fixtures, signage, tools |
| **Terrazzo** | Accent surfaces, counters |
| **Matte ceramic** | Product vessels, soak bowls |
| **Uncoated paper** | Business cards, menus, gift cards |
| **Warm wood** | Reception, shelving, furniture |

### 8.2 Digital Textures

- **Background noise:** Subtle grain overlay at 2–5% opacity on digital backgrounds
- **Border treatment:** Thin solid strokes (`1px` or `1pt`) — never border-radius
- **Shadows:** Warm-toned box shadows using `rgba(88, 70, 56, 0.12)` — never cool gray shadows

### 8.3 Pattern Use

- **Mono line rules:** 80px wide, 1px high, Bronze color — use as section dividers
- **Grid overlays:** Subtle 1px grid at low opacity for certain editorial moments
- **No repeating patterns** — the brand does not use wallpaper-style patterns

## 09 — Digital Applications

### 9.1 Website (v1 Static / v2 Next.js)

The site follows a single-page editorial format with:

| Element | Treatment |
|---|---|
| **Header** | Fixed, transparent-to-cream on scroll, 0.95 backdrop-blur |
| **Navigation** | Full-screen mega menu with image panel |
| **Cards** | Thin border, no radius, hover darkens border to solid |
| **Buttons** | Monospace uppercase, solid bronze or outline, no radius, 0.75rem padding |
| **Forms** | Transparent background, thin bronze border, mono labels |
| **Modals** | Backdrop blur overlay, centered card with mono header |
| **Loading** | Shimmer skeleton animation for service/gallery cards |

### 9.2 Animation Principles

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Base transition** | `300ms cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)` |
| **Scroll reveal** | Fade-up 35px, 1.1s cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) |
| **Menu overlay** | Opacity + visibility, 0.5s ease |
| **Calendar panel** | Slide-in 0.4s ease |
| **Hover states** | Border darkens, background tints, 0.25s ease |

### 9.3 Responsive Breakpoints

| Breakpoint | Target |
|---|---|
| **≥ 1025px** | Desktop — full mega menu, multi-column grids |
| **769–1024px** | Tablet — collapsed nav, 2-col grids |
| **481–768px** | Mobile — hamburger nav, single-col, sticky book CTA |
| **≤ 480px** | Small phone — stacked layout, hidden step labels |

### 9.4 Booking System UI Patterns

- Step indicator: 5-step mono progress bar with numbered circles
- Selection cards: Tappable bordered cards with active state (filled bronze)
- Calendar: Custom-built with disabled days (Sun/Mon), 60-day lookahead
- Time slots: Grid of 1-hour buttons with waitlist option for booked slots
- Confirmation: Receipt-style modal with download ICS and preparation list

## 11 — Social Media

### 11.1 Grid Aesthetic

- **Palette:** 80% cream/beige tones, 15% bronze text, 5% editorial color
- **Texture:** Slight film grain, warm desaturated tones
- **Layout:** Alternating product shots, detail macros, and text-on-cream cards
- **No:** Bright filters, hard shadows, busy compositions, hashtag-heavy captions

### 11.2 Instagram Feed Strategy

| Content Type | Frequency | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Nail Art Detail** | 40% | Macro close-ups of gold foil, chrome, linework |
| **Atmosphere** | 25% | Salon interior, natural light, material details |
| **Educational** | 15% | Technique spotlights (Russian manicure, Gel-X process) |
| **Text Cards** | 10% | Typographic quotes, service announcements on cream background |
| **Client / Editorial** | 10% | Reposted client tags, editorial collaborations |

### 11.3 Caption Tone

- Short (1–3 sentences max on feed)
- Longer-form for carousels (step-by-step)
- No emojis except ✦ or ─ as decorative spacers
- Formatting: Single line breaks between phrases, no hashtag blocks (1–3 max at end)

### 11.4 Profile

- **Avatar:** Logo mark on cream (square crop)
- **Bio format:** "3636 McKinney Ave, Uptown Dallas \_ Bespoke nail artistry \_ Tue–Sat 10–7 \_ Book via link"
- **Link:** Direct to booking (v2), or link-in-bio tool

## 14 — Do's & Don'ts (Quick Reference)

| Context | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| Logo | Use full-color on cream | Place on busy photography |
| Colors | Stay within brand palette | Introduce cool tones or black |
| Typography | Use three approved typefaces | Use system fonts, decorative, or variable fonts |
| Imagery | Shoot warm, raw, editorial | Use stock photos or flash photography |
| Voice | Write sparse, sensory, precise | Write corporate, wordy, or generic |
| UI | Thin borders, no radius, mono labels | Round corners, shadows, or gradients |
| Social | Warm desaturated grid, text cards | Bright filters, emoji-heavy, hashtag spam |
| Services | Use real names and real prices | Replace with placeholder copy |
